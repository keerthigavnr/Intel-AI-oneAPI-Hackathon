Sexual Domestic Abuse Support

If you are experiencing sexual domestic abuse, know that it is not your fault and you are not alone. Help is available, and you have the right to seek safety and support.

Sexual domestic abuse refers to any form of non-consensual sexual activity or behavior occurring within an intimate relationship, encompassing a range of actions from verbal harassment to physical assault.

Understanding Sexual Domestic Abuse

Sexual domestic abuse is a form of domestic violence and sexual violence, and it can significantly impact your physical, emotional, and mental health. It includes but is not limited to:

  • Forced or coerced engagement in sexual acts
  • Sexual contact without consent
  • Sexual harassment, including unwanted touching and verbal harassment
  • Using threats or manipulation to obtain sexual acts
  • Sexually degrading treatment

Recognizing Signs of Sexual Domestic Abuse

Recognizing sexual domestic abuse is crucial for seeking help. It can be challenging to identify, especially when the abuser is someone close or intimate. Signs include but are not limited to:

  • Feeling pressured or coerced into sexual activity
  • Experiencing unwanted sexual comments or advances
  • Being threatened or manipulated into sexual activity
  • Physical injuries resulting from sexual assault
  • Feeling fear or anxiety around the abuser

Resources and Support

Various organizations and hotlines are dedicated to supporting victims of sexual domestic abuse. They can provide confidential advice, shelter, counseling, and legal assistance. It's important to reach out when you feel safe to do so.

  • National Domestic Violence Hotline
  • Local shelters and crisis centers
  • Sexual assault support services
  • Legal aid organizations specializing in domestic abuse
