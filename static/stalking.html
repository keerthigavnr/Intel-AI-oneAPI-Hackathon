Stalking Support

If you are experiencing stalking, know that you are not alone, and support is available to help you through this challenging time. Stalking is a serious and frightening experience, and it's important to take steps to ensure your safety.

Stalking involves unwanted and repeated attention, harassment, contact, or any other behavior directed at you without your consent, causing fear or distress.

Understanding Stalking

Stalking can take various forms, including but not limited to:

  • Unwanted and persistent communication (calls, texts, emails)
  • Following or tracking your movements
  • Showing up at your home, workplace, or social events uninvited
  • Spreading false rumors about you
  • Monitoring your online activities
  • Leaving unwanted gifts or messages

Signs of Being Stalked

It's important to be aware of signs that you may be a target of stalking:

  • Receiving excessive, unwanted attention
  • Feeling constantly watched or followed
  • Discovering signs of someone entering your home or property without permission
  • Receiving persistent and intrusive communication
  • Experiencing a sense of fear or unease

Resources and Getting Help

If you or someone you know is experiencing stalking, there are resources available to help you take action and ensure your safety:

  • Contact local law enforcement to report incidents
  • Obtain a restraining order or protection order
  • Document incidents and keep evidence of stalking behavior
  • Seek support from local victim advocacy organizations

Remember, reaching out for help is a crucial step toward protecting yourself and finding support.
