Physical Abuse Support

We've listened to your situation, and we want to acknowledge that you might be going through a challenging time. Your feelings are valid, and we are here to offer support and resources.

If you have experienced physical abuse, know that you are not alone. Your safety and well-being matter, and there is help available for you.

Understanding Physical Abuse

Physical abuse can take various forms, including:

  • Grabbing
  • Pushing
  • Slapping
  • Shoving
  • Hitting
  • Stabbing
  • Burning
  • Biting

Other forms of physical abuse may involve withholding physical needs, refusing to release necessities, locking a victim out of the house, or withholding help when the victim is sick or injured.

Recognizing Signs of Physical Abuse

It is still considered physical abuse even if the incidents seem minor or have occurred one or two times in the relationship. If you have experienced physical assault, there is help available.

What to Do if You've Experienced Physical Abuse

Your safety is a top priority. Consider taking the following steps:

  • Reach out to a trusted friend, family member, or counselor.
  • Contact local law enforcement for immediate assistance.
  • Seek medical attention for any injuries.
  • Explore resources and support groups for survivors of abuse.

Remember, you are not alone, and there are people and organizations ready to support you on your journey to healing.
