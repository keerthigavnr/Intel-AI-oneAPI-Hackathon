Emotional Abuse Support

We understand that emotional abuse can be a deeply distressing experience, and we want to acknowledge your feelings. You are not alone, and support is available for you.

Emotional abuse is a type of domestic violence that targets the emotional and psychological well-being of a person. It can take various forms, including gaslighting, threats, excessive demands, constant criticism, and more.

Understanding Emotional Abuse

Emotional abuse uses words and actions to berate, embarrass, or pull down the self-esteem of another person. Examples of emotional abuse include:

  • Gaslighting
  • Direct threats of physical harm
  • Indirect threats to hurt loved ones
  • Making excessive, unreasonable demands
  • Invalidating the victim's feelings
  • Constant criticism and name-calling
  • Creating chaos
  • Guilt-tripping or emotionally blackmailing
  • Giving the silent treatment
  • Controlling and isolating the victim
  • Threatening self-harm to manipulate the victim
  • Ignoring the person's needs or requests
  • Attacking their self-worth

Recognizing Signs of Emotional Abuse

Emotional abuse goes beyond intimate relationships and can affect spouses, children, and relatives. Not all abusive relationships involve physical violence, and emotional abuse is often minimized or overlooked.

The Impact of Emotional Abuse

The scars of emotional abuse are real and can run deep. Emotional abuse aims to chip away at your feelings of self-worth and independence, leaving you feeling trapped. It includes verbal abuse, isolation, intimidation, and controlling behavior.

If you are experiencing emotional abuse, there are resources and support networks available to help you regain control and find a path to healing.
